## The 72-Hour Pre-Launch Window

### 72 Hours Before: Build Your Hunter Network

This is the step everyone skips.

ProductHunt's ranking algorithm weighs **early upvotes** heavily. The first 2-3 hours determine whether you hit the front page.

Your job: line up 15-25 people who will upvote within the first hour.

**How:**
1. DM everyone in your network who's active on PH
2. Join PH-related Slack/Discord communities
3. Post in Indie Hackers: "Launching on PH Tuesday — would love your support"
4. Schedule your maker comment to post at 00:01 PST

**Don't:** Buy upvotes. Don't ask random people. PH detects manipulation and will bury you.

### 48 Hours Before: Prepare Your On-Site Conversion Path

Here's what most people miss: **ProductHunt drives traffic. Your landing page converts it.**

If your redirect URL goes to your homepage, you've lost.

Create a dedicated launch landing page with:
- Hero section that matches your PH listing copy
- Social proof (even if it's just "Join 50+ founders already using...")
- Clear CTA above the fold
- Pricing visible without scrolling
- Email capture for people who aren't ready to buy

### 24 Hours Before: Finalize Your Maker Comment

The maker comment is the **most important 500 characters on your entire launch**. It's the first thing people read after your product description.

Template that works:

```
Hey PH —

[One sentence about what you built and why it matters]

[2-3 bullet points of key features/benefits]

Launch pricing: $X (regular $Y) — today only.

I'd love honest feedback. What would make this even better?

— [Your name/team]
```

Notice: no apologies, no "sorry for the self-promotion," no emojis in professional contexts. Confidence, not humility.

## Launch Day Hour-by-Hour

### Hour 0-1 (00:01-01:01 PST): The Critical Window

- Product goes live at 00:01 PST (08:01 UTC)
- Post your maker comment within 60 seconds
- Activate your hunter network: send the link NOW
- **Do not optimize your listing during this hour.** You can't edit after posting (well, you can, but algorithm penalizes changes during ranking)

### Hour 1-3: Respond to Every Comment

Every. Single. Comment.

PH algorithm tracks engagement. Products with active makers rank higher.

Reply within 15 minutes. Be specific. Ask follow-up questions. Show you're present.

**Template:**
> "Great question, [name]. [Answer]. Would [alternative] be more useful for your use case?"

### Hour 3-6: Second Wave — External Traffic

Now you push to external channels (without being spammy):

1. **Reddit** — Post in relevant subreddits. Provide genuine value. Mention your PH launch only in context.
2. **Indie Hackers** — Build-in-public post featuring your PH journey.
3. **Email list** — "We just launched on PH and would love your support."
4. **Twitter/X** — Thread about the launch story.

### Hour 6-12: Sustain Momentum

- Continue responding to comments
- Share launch metrics publicly ("We just crossed 200 upvotes!")
- Ask satisfied early users to leave reviews

### Hour 12-24: The Long Tail

- Reply to late-comers
- Thank everyone publicly
- Start planning post-launch content

## The Metrics That Matter

Most founders chase upvotes. That's vanity.

**What actually matters:**

| Metric | Good | Great | Exceptional |
|--------|------|-------|-------------|
| Upvotes | 200+ | 500+ | 1000+ |
| Comments | 30+ | 75+ | 150+ |
| Clicks to site | 1,000+ | 3,000+ | 8,000+ |
| Conversion rate | 2%+ | 5%+ | 10%+ |
| Revenue Day 1 | $100+ | $500+ | $2,000+ |

If you get 1,000 upvotes but 0 revenue, you failed.

**Optimize for conversions, not visibility.**

## Post-Launch: Don't Ghost

The day after your launch:

1. **Publish a retrospective blog post** — "What launching on ProductHunt taught us about [your niche]"
2. **Update your PH gallery** — Add new screenshots based on feedback
3. **Follow up with every person who commented** — Turn PH visitors into customers
4. **Analyze what worked** — Double down on channels that drove conversions

## Common Mistakes That Kill Launches

### 1. No Pre-Launch Audience
If you go to PH with zero followers and zero network, you're starting from nothing. Build your audience 2-3 weeks before.

### 2. Weak Thumbnail
Your thumbnail is your first impression at the size of a postage stamp. High contrast. One focal point. No clutter.

### 3. Feature-Dump Description
Don't list 47 features. Lead with the ONE transformation your product enables. Everything else is support.

### 4. Ignoring Comments for Hours
The maker who disappears after posting kills their own ranking. Be present for 12+ hours.

### 5. No CTA in the Listing
"Check it out" is not a CTA. "Get 10 production-ready templates for $29 (today only)" is.
