## 🚀 Hosting & Deployment

### 1. Vercel — Best for Frontend & Full-Stack

**Pricing:** Free tier available · Pro $20/mo · Enterprise custom

Vercel is our go-to for deploying Next.js, React, and static sites. The developer experience is unmatched — push to GitHub, and your site is live in seconds with automatic previews for every PR.

**Pros:**
- Zero-config deployments for Next.js
- Edge functions with global CDN
- Automatic HTTPS and custom domains
- Incredible preview deployments for teams
- Analytics and performance monitoring built-in

**Cons:**
- Can get expensive at scale (bandwidth costs)
- Serverless functions have execution time limits
- Vendor lock-in with Next.js-specific features

**Our rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)

👉 **[Deploy on Vercel →](https://vercel.com)** (Free tier available)

### 2. Railway — Best for Full-Stack & Databases

**Pricing:** Starter $5/mo · Pro $20/mo · Custom for teams

Railway is the easiest way to deploy a full-stack app with a database. Think of it as Heroku reimagined for 2026 — you get a Postgres database, Redis, and your app in one click.

**Pros:**
- One-click database provisioning
- Beautiful dashboard and CLI
- Supports any language/framework
- Great for prototyping and MVPs
- Built-in environment variables management

**Cons:**
- Less mature than AWS/GCP for enterprise
- Pricing can be unpredictable for variable workloads
- Limited regions compared to major clouds

**Our rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐½ (4.5/5)

👉 **[Deploy on Railway →](https://railway.app)** (Starter from $5/mo)

### 3. DigitalOcean — Best for Cloud Infrastructure

**Pricing:** Droplets from $4/mo · Kubernetes from $12/mo · Generous free credit for startups

DigitalOcean is the developer-friendly cloud. No surprise bills, clear pricing, and excellent documentation. We use it for production workloads that need dedicated resources.

**Pros:**
- Simple, predictable pricing
- Excellent documentation and tutorials
- Managed Kubernetes, databases, and storage
- 200+ one-click app deployments
- Strong community and support

**Cons:**
- Fewer services than AWS/GCP
- No serverless compute option (yet)
- Support can be slow on lower tiers

**Our rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐½ (4.5/5)

Cloudflare is non-negotiable for any serious project. Free CDN, DNS, SSL, DDoS protection, and now Workers (serverless at the edge). We put every ZOO project behind Cloudflare.

## 🗄️ Backend & Database

### 5. Supabase — Best Open-Source Firebase Alternative

**Pricing:** Free tier · Pro $25/mo · Team $599/mo

Supabase gives you a full Postgres database with real-time subscriptions, authentication, storage, and edge functions. It's the backend we recommend to every startup we work with.

**Pros:**
- Full Postgres (not a toy database)
- Real-time subscriptions out of the box
- Built-in auth with multiple providers
- Auto-generated REST and GraphQL APIs
- Storage with image transformation
- Open-source and self-hostable

**Cons:**
- Can be slow on complex queries
- Free tier has pausing after inactivity
- Edge functions still maturing

**Our rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)

👉 **[Start with Supabase →](https://supabase.com)** (Free tier available)

### 6. Stripe — Best for Payments

**Pricing:** 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction · No monthly fee

Stripe is the gold standard for online payments. Whether you're building a SaaS, marketplace, or e-commerce site, Stripe handles it all — subscriptions, one-time payments, invoicing, and more.

**Pros:**
- Best-in-class developer experience
- Comprehensive API and SDKs
- Handles subscriptions, invoicing, tax
- Global coverage (135+ currencies)
- Radar for fraud detection

**Cons:**
- Fees add up for high-volume businesses
- Account holds can be frustrating
- Complex pricing for advanced features

**Our rating:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)

👉 **[Start accepting payments with Stripe →](https://stripe.com)** (Pay-as-you-go)
