How to Launch on ProductHunt: The Definitive Playbook

From a team launching May 12 — the 72-hour pre-launch window, hour-by-hour timeline, metrics that matter, and the mistakes that kill most launches.

What Most Founders Get Wrong About ProductHunt

Most founders treat ProductHunt like a "post and pray" platform. They write a quick description, upload a thumbnail, hit submit at midnight San Francisco time, and wonder why they end up with 47 upvotes and zero conversions.

That's not a launch. That's a checkbox.

After preparing multiple ProductHunt launches (including ours — ZOO Landing Page Templates go live May 12), here's the playbook that actually works.

The 72-Hour Pre-Launch Window

72 Hours Before: Build Your Hunter Network

This is the step everyone skips. ProductHunt's ranking algorithm weighs early upvotes heavily. The first 2-3 hours determine whether you hit the front page. Your job: line up 15-25 people who will upvote within the first hour.

How:

  • DM everyone in your network who's active on PH
  • Join PH-related Slack/Discord communities
  • Post in Indie Hackers: "Launching on PH Tuesday — would love your support"
  • Schedule your maker comment to post at 00:01 PST

Don't: Buy upvotes. Don't ask random people. PH detects manipulation and will bury you.

48 Hours Before: Prepare Your On-Site Conversion Path

Here's what people miss: ProductHunt drives traffic. Your landing page converts it. If your redirect URL goes to your homepage, you've lost.

Create a dedicated launch landing page with hero section matching your PH copy, social proof, clear CTA above the fold, pricing visible without scrolling, and email capture for people not ready to buy.

24 Hours Before: Finalize Your Maker Comment

The maker comment is the most important 500 characters on your entire launch. It's the first thing people read after your product description. Lead with value, list key benefits, state launch pricing clearly, and ask for honest feedback. No apologies.

Launch Day Hour-by-Hour

Hour 0-1 (00:01-01:01 PST): The Critical Window

  • Product goes live at 00:01 PST (08:01 UTC)
  • Post your maker comment within 60 seconds
  • Activate your hunter network: send the link NOW
  • Do not optimize your listing during this hour.

Hour 1-3: Respond to Every Comment

Every. Single. Comment. PH algorithm tracks engagement. Products with active makers rank higher. Reply within 15 minutes. Be specific. Ask follow-up questions. Show you're present.

Hour 3-6: Second Wave — External Traffic

Push to external channels without being spammy: Reddit in relevant subreddits (value-first), Indie Hackers build-in-public post, email list announcement, and Twitter/X thread about the launch story.

Hour 6-12: Sustain Momentum

Continue responding to comments, share launch metrics publicly ("We just crossed 200 upvotes!"), ask satisfied early users to leave reviews.

Hour 12-24: The Long Tail

Reply to late-comers, thank everyone publicly, start planning post-launch content.

The Metrics That Matter

Most founders chase upvotes. That's vanity. What actually matters:

MetricGoodGreatExceptional
Upvotes200+500+1,000+
Comments30+75+150+
Clicks to site1,000+3,000+8,000+
Conversion rate2%+5%+10%+
Revenue Day 1$100+$500+$2,000+

If you get 1,000 upvotes but 0 revenue, you failed. Optimize for conversions, not visibility.

Post-Launch: Don't Ghost

The day after your launch: publish a retrospective blog post, update your PH gallery based on feedback, follow up with every person who commented, and analyze what channels drove conversions.

Common Mistakes That Kill Launches

  1. No Pre-Launch Audience — Build your network 2-3 weeks before.
  2. Weak Thumbnail — High contrast, one focal point, no clutter. It displays postage-stamp sized.
  3. Feature-Dump Description — Lead with ONE transformation, not 47 features.
  4. Ignoring Comments for Hours — Be present for 12+ hours.
  5. No CTA in the Listing — "Check it out" is not a CTA. State value + price + urgency.
